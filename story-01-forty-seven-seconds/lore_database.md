## 0. The One-Sentence Engine

A man whose job is to *trust recordings more than memory* discovers that the recordings have begun trusting **something else** — and the only place the truth might live is the 47 seconds no recording will keep.

---

## 1. THE LAW OF THE GAP (Hard Canon)

These rules are inviolable. The horror works *only* if they are never broken on a whim.

1. **Duration is exactly 47 seconds.** Never 46, never 48. When evidence shows "≈47," that "almost" is itself a clue (it means the gap is *leaking* into a record it shouldn't).
2. **The gap is centred on the narrator.** Whatever frame he occupies, the missing window brackets him.
3. **He never remembers it.** No flashbacks, no recovered memory. He only ever *reconstructs* — from reflections, witnesses, moved objects, metadata.
4. **Reconstruction always worsens the picture.** Every method he invents to "catch" the gap produces a *larger* contradiction than the method before.
5. **The record degrades upward.** Personal devices → networked/cloud → other people's devices → public infrastructure → lived reality. (See §3.)
6. **The Incomplete Door and the number 47 recur inside evidence** — never narrated as "supernatural," only *found*. (See §4.)
7. **No theory is ever confirmed.** Canon must remain compatible with all four explanations at all times. (See §6.)

---

## 2. WHY 47? (In-World Logic — kept deliberately unresolved)

The story offers the audience *fuel* for "why 47," and refuses to settle it:

- **Medical fuel:** 47 seconds is plausibly the length of a complex partial seizure + post-ictal confabulation window. (A neurologist says this on-page.)
- **Technological fuel:** the lab's restoration model uses a rolling **47-frame** predictive buffer; at the project's frame-rate, the buffer flush time is read aloud as "about forty-seven seconds." (A coincidence — or the cause.)
- **Criminal fuel:** 47 seconds is exactly how long it takes to walk from the narrator's desk to the lab's only blind corridor and back — the one stretch with no camera.
- **Distortion fuel:** the recurring **2:47** and **Door** suggest 47 is not a duration at all but an *address*.

**Canon stance:** all four "whys" are seeded; none is privileged.

## 3. THE ESCALATION LADDER (Act Architecture)

Each stage must (a) widen *who/what* the gap affects, and (b) deliver one **anchor impossible exhibit** that contradicts the prior stage harder.

### ACT I — PERSONAL ANOMALY
- **Scope:** only the narrator's own phone clips and voice memos.
- **Feel:** "I'm tired / I'm sick." Easy to dismiss.
- **Anchor exhibit (E1):** He vividly remembers locking the lab; the only clip of that moment jumps 47s and the lock is *already* engaged before he reaches it. (False memory vs missing action.)
- **Contradiction level:** 1 — *memory vs footage.*

### ACT II — DIGITAL ANOMALY
- **Scope:** CCTV, cloud backups, a stranger's unrelated livestream — all carry the *identical* 47s hole, same wall-clock window.
- **Feel:** "It's not me. It's the record." Worse, because it's external.
- **Anchor exhibit (E2 — pre-cognition):** A photo auto-backed-up and **timestamped 47s before** a cup falls already shows the cup shattered on the floor. The metadata is clean. (Effect precedes cause.)
- **Contradiction level:** 2 — *the record contradicts causality.*

### ACT III — SOCIAL ANOMALY
- **Scope:** other people. Two witnesses to the same 47s remember **mutually exclusive** events; people who shared a gap with him grow cold and avoid him.
- **Feel:** "Reality's witnesses don't agree." Isolation sets in.
- **Anchor exhibit (E3 — two-place object):** His keys are recorded by **Camera A** on his desk and **Camera B** at the lobby turnstile **at the same second** — before he has moved them at all. Both files validate. (One object, two truths.)
- **Contradiction level:** 3 — *the record contradicts physical singularity.*

### ACT IV — REALITY ANOMALY
- **Scope:** lived experience. He *feels* a 47s hole happen live (a clock jumps, no one else noticed); he finds himself somewhere he didn't walk to; the **Incomplete Door** appears as a physical thing in a corridor that has no door.
- **Feel:** the record was the warning; now the world is doing it.
- **Anchor exhibit (E4 — the captured gap):** The one recording engineered to *survive* the gap plays the full 47s. The room is exactly as it is — **except he is not in it**, and 9 seconds in, something **off-frame exhales**. The final still, printed before he set the trap, already shows this frame.
- **Contradiction level:** 4 — *the record contradicts his existence.*

## 4. THE RECURRING SYMBOL — THE INCOMPLETE DOOR

**Definition:** a too-narrow door, slightly ajar, opening on absolute black, standing where no door belongs. It is *never* the subject of a shot; it is always **found in the margins** of evidence.

**Placement rules (for all later visual files):**
- It must appear **only inside evidence** (a frame, a reflection, a photo background, a dead monitor), never as a "scare reveal."
- It is usually paired with a **2:47** readout or a **47** (frame number, file length `00:00:47`, room/locker "47", page 47).
- It grows by **degree, not by jump:** Act I it's a sliver in one reflection; Act IV it's a full doorway in a real corridor.
- Viewers should be trainable to *hunt* for it → replay & Reddit fuel.

**Universe note:** the Door and the 2:47 motif belong to **THE ALLEY VERSE** line and recur across its stories. They are **not** shared with the separate *वो गली* franchise. Within Story_01 they are presented so a newcomer reads them as eerie coincidence and a returning fan reads them as canon.

## 6. THE FOUR EXPLANATIONS (Finale Logic)

The final act must **strengthen all four simultaneously** and confirm none. Each gets: a champion (a character or document that argues it), a hard exhibit, and a fatal gap (the reason it can't fully win).

| Explanation | Champion | Hard exhibit | Fatal gap (why it can't close) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MEDICAL** | the neurologist | X2 (EEG), E1 | can't explain E2/E3 appearing on *other people's* devices |
| **CRIMINAL** | the laid-off colleague | X1 (deleted user), footprints | can't fake E2's pre-event timestamp on a sealed cloud |
| **TECHNOLOGICAL** | the model's own logs | X3 (47-frame buffer) | the gap appears on analog/older cameras the model never touched |
| **REALITY-DISTORTION** | the Door / E4 | X4, E4 | rests on the one thing that can't be re-checked — he can't re-watch E4 the same way twice |

**The final exhibit (the "all-four amplifier"):** the printed still that shows E4 *before* he built the trap. To the Medical camp it's misremembered print order; to the Criminal camp it's a planted print; to the Technological camp it's the model leaking its prediction; to the Distortion camp it's prophecy. Same object, four airtight readings.

**Closing question (canon-mandated last line theme):**
> *"What if the missing forty-seven seconds were the only real part?"*

## 7. COMMENT-WAR MOMENTS (must survive into story + edit)

1. **E3 / two-place keys** — viewers will pause both timestamps to "win." Keep both timestamps legible and identical on screen.
2. **The mutually exclusive witnesses (Act III)** — two coworkers, two incompatible memories of the same 47s; the story must refuse to flag which is false.
3. **E4 / the empty room + the breath** — *why* is he absent, and *whose* breath is it? Leave four readings open.

> A fourth, quieter debate (bonus): is the narrator even narrating in order? (See §9 unreliability.)

## 8. TIMELINE (Story-internal, reconstructed — itself disputed)

Presented as the narrator's own evidence board. Times cluster on **:47** and **2:47** on purpose.

- **Day 0 — 14:47** — first noticed gap (a selfie video for a friend). Dismissed.
- **Day 2 — 02:47** — first *night* gap; wakes standing in the kitchen; clip from the fridge-cam jumps 47s.
- **Day 5** — E1 confirmed (the lock). Books a doctor.
- **Day 6 — 02:47** — E2 (pre-cognitive cup). Stops trusting cloud time.
- **Day 9** — X2 (EEG): a 47-second notation the technician "doesn't remember typing."
- **Day 11** — E3 (two-place keys). A coworker stops speaking to him.
- **Day 12** — the two witnesses; X1 (deleted user) surfaces.
- **Day 14 — 02:47** — X3 (the model's buffer flag). He builds the trap.
- **Day 15 — 02:47:00–02:47:47** — E4. The story ends mid-reconstruction.

**Canon caveat:** the narrator admits, late, that he can no longer be sure the days happened in this order — three exhibits are dated *before* he says he obtained them.

## 9. THE NARRATOR'S UNRELIABILITY (Tier-1 device)

- He is a *professional* at spotting fakery — so when *he* is fooled, the audience trusts the fooling.
- He over-documents (timestamps everything) — which is exactly how the contradictions become legible to us.
- He withholds one thing until Act IV: he has done this job — chasing a 47-second gap — **once before**, on someone else's footage, years ago. He never says what happened to that person. (Seed for Story_02 / franchise.)
